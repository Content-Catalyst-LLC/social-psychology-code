# Measurement Notes

## Core norm model

A behavior is norm-governed when a person's preference for compliance is conditional on:

1. empirical expectations about what others do
2. normative expectations about what others believe should be done
3. sanctions or rewards attached to compliance or deviation
4. identification with the relevant reference group

## Descriptive and injunctive norms

Descriptive norm perception:

D_i = perceived proportion of relevant others who perform the behavior

Injunctive norm perception:

J_i = perceived proportion of relevant others who approve of the behavior

Combined norm salience:

N_i = w_D * D_i + w_J * J_i

## Compliance propensity

Compliance propensity can be represented as:

C* = beta0
   + beta1*DescriptiveNorm
   + beta2*InjunctiveNorm
   + beta3*EmpiricalExpectation
   + beta4*NormativeExpectation
   + beta5*SanctionSalience
   + beta6*ReferenceGroupIdentification
   + beta7*InstitutionalLegitimacy
   + beta8*DynamicNormTrend
   - beta9*PersonalCost
   + beta10*PersonalAttitude

## Pluralistic ignorance

Pluralistic ignorance occurs when:

private_attitude_i > perceived_group_approval_i

or when actors believe a group supports a norm more strongly than members privately do.

## Threshold model

An actor complies when expected compliance among others crosses a personal threshold:

a_i = comply if p_hat >= tau_i

where:

- p_hat = perceived compliance of others
- tau_i = individual threshold

## Recommended reliability checks

- Descriptive norm scale reliability
- Injunctive norm scale reliability
- Empirical expectation reliability
- Normative expectation reliability
- Sanction salience reliability
- Reference-group identification reliability
- Institutional legitimacy reliability

## Recommended validity checks

- Verify norm-message manipulation changed norm perceptions.
- Test descriptive and injunctive norms separately.
- Test whether norm salience moderates behavior change.
- Test whether reference-group identification moderates norm effects.
- Test for boomerang effects after descriptive norm messages.
- Test whether injunctive cues reduce destructive boomerang effects.
- Test whether private attitudes diverge from perceived group norms.
