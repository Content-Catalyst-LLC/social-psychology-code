# Measurement Notes

## Comparison gap

G_i = S_i - R_i

where S_i is perceived self-standing and R_i is perceived reference standing.

## Self-evaluation change

DeltaE_i = E_post,i - E_pre,i

## Direction coding

- upward: R_i > S_i
- downward: R_i < S_i
- lateral: R_i ≈ S_i

## Attainability moderation

DeltaE_i = beta0 + beta1 * upward_i + beta2 * attainability_i
           + beta3 * upward_i * attainability_i + error_i

High attainability may turn upward comparison into inspiration or motivation rather than discouragement.

## Identity relevance

Comparison effects are usually stronger when the domain matters to the self-concept.

## Digital comparison exposure

Digital comparison measures should distinguish:

- passive exposure
- active searching
- curated target visibility
- appearance comparison
- achievement comparison
- status comparison
- social approval metrics
- algorithmic repetition

## Relative deprivation

RD_i = perceived_gap_i * perceived_deservingness_i * emotional_grievance_i

## Recommended reliability checks

- Social comparison orientation scales
- Self-esteem and self-evaluation scales
- Affect scales
- Envy/inspiration scales
- Relative deprivation scales
- Norm perception measures
- Temporal stability in diary designs

## Recommended validity checks

- Distinguish actual status from perceived comparison gap
- Compare self-report affect with behavioral motivation
- Test whether similarity changes comparison effects
- Test whether attainability moderates upward comparison
- Test whether domain importance moderates affect
- Test repeated exposure versus one-time comparison
