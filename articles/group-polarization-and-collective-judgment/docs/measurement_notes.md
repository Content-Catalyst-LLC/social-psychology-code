# Measurement Notes

## Directional polarization

Let x_i0 be pre-discussion attitude and x_i1 post-discussion attitude.

Group mean before discussion:

xbar0 = mean(x_i0)

Group mean after discussion:

xbar1 = mean(x_i1)

Directional polarization occurs when:

abs(xbar1) > abs(xbar0)

and sign(xbar1) == sign(xbar0)

## Individual attitude shift

shift_i = post_attitude_i - pre_attitude_i

## Extremity amplification

extremity_shift_i = abs(post_attitude_i) - abs(pre_attitude_i)

## Confidence amplification

confidence_shift_i = post_confidence_i - pre_confidence_i

## Collective judgment quality

decision_quality can rise or fall after group discussion.

Improvement is possible when:
- diverse information is pooled
- dissent is protected
- evidence is reviewed independently
- minority views are taken seriously
- groups do not reward identity performance over accuracy

Judgment failure is more likely when:
- groups are homogeneous
- norms punish dissent
- discussion rewards certainty
- arguments are one-sided
- identity salience is high
- reputational incentives favor extremity
- leaders frame moderation as weakness

## Recommended validity checks

- Confirm discussion changed perceived argument exposure.
- Confirm homogeneity condition altered informational diversity.
- Confirm identity salience manipulation increased group-based self-categorization.
- Confirm dissent intervention increased perceived dissent quality.
- Test whether discussion increased confidence independently of accuracy.
- Test whether structured deliberation reduces polarization without suppressing legitimate disagreement.
