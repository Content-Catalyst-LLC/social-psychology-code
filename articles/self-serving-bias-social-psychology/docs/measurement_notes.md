# Measurement Notes

## Basic self-serving bias score

SSB_i = I_success,i - I_failure,i

where I_success is internal attribution after success and I_failure is internal attribution after failure.

## Full attributional asymmetry score

SSB*_i = (I_success,i - I_failure,i) + (E_failure,i - E_success,i)

where E_failure is external attribution after failure and E_success is external attribution after success.

## Responsibility asymmetry

RA_i = Responsibility_success,i - Responsibility_failure,i

## Credit/blame asymmetry

CBA_i = Credit_success,i - Blame_failure,i

This may be used when the design measures credit and blame separately.

## Accountability moderation

SSB_i = beta0 + beta1 * ego_threat_i - beta2 * accountability_i
        + beta3 * task_importance_i + error_i

## Evidence strength

External attribution is not automatically biased. Strong evidence of situational constraint should increase external attribution. A research design should measure evidence strength and test whether attribution remains asymmetrical after evidence is held constant.

## Learning intention

A strong self-serving bias may reduce learning intention when failure is deflected externally.

## Recommended reliability checks

- Attribution scale reliability
- Responsibility/blame scale reliability
- Self-esteem and ego-threat scales
- Accountability pressure manipulation checks
- Response-time filtering rules

## Recommended validity checks

- Compare self outcomes with identical other outcomes
- Separate actual evidence from motivated interpretation
- Test positive and negative valence symmetrically
- Test success credit and failure blame separately
- Include ambiguous and unambiguous outcome conditions
- Include cultural/self-construal measures when relevant
