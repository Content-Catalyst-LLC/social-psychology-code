# Measurement Notes

## Bayesian attribution

P(h | x) = P(x | h)P(h) / P(x)

## Disposition-situation weighting

Attribution = wD * DispositionalEvidence + wS * SituationalEvidence

## Covariation judgment

Situational attribution tends to rise when consensus, distinctiveness, and consistency are high.

## Responsibility judgment

Responsibility = f(Intentionality, Choice, Controllability, Foreseeability, Harm, SituationalConstraint)

## Achievement attribution

ExpectationNext = ExpectationNow + alpha * (CauseStability - ExpectationNow)

## Hostile attribution

HostileAttribution = f(Ambiguity, ThreatSensitivity, PriorConflict, OutgroupCue, LowContextInformation)

## Institutional blame model

BlameIndividual = beta0
  + beta1*ActorSalience
  + beta2*Intentionality
  + beta3*Controllability
  - beta4*SituationalConstraint
  - beta5*SystemVisibility
  + error

## Recommended validity checks

- Confirm that consensus, distinctiveness, and consistency manipulations are perceived correctly.
- Confirm that ambiguity manipulations alter uncertainty.
- Distinguish internal attribution from controllability.
- Test whether responsibility is mediated by controllability and intentionality.
- Test actor-observer asymmetry with matched events.
- Test whether situational information reduces correspondence bias.
- Test whether accountability increases system-level attribution in institutional cases.
