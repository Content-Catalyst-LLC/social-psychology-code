# Measurement Notes

## Drive-theory formulation

A_t = A_0 + alpha * SocialPresence + gamma * EvaluationPressure

where:

- A_t = arousal at trial t
- A_0 = baseline arousal
- SocialPresence = audience or coaction presence
- EvaluationPressure = perceived likelihood of being judged

## Dominant response

Performance improvement depends on whether the dominant response is correct:

Performance = BaselineSkill + beta_1*Arousal*DominantCorrect - beta_2*Arousal*(1 - DominantCorrect)

## Task difficulty interaction

Social facilitation is expected when task difficulty is low or mastery is high.

Social inhibition is expected when task difficulty is high or mastery is low.

## Evaluation apprehension

Evaluation pressure can be measured through:

- perceived scrutiny
- expected judgment
- concern about embarrassment
- reputational stakes
- observer expertise
- recording/monitoring awareness

## Distraction-conflict

Effective capacity can be modeled as:

Capacity_effective = Capacity - delta * DistractionIndex

where distraction increases with audience salience, comparison pressure, and attentional conflict.

## Digital monitoring

Digital monitoring should be separated into:

- objective monitoring condition
- awareness of monitoring
- perceived fairness of monitoring
- control over monitoring
- feedback availability
- social support

## Recommended reliability checks

- Evaluation apprehension scale reliability
- Social anxiety scale reliability
- Perceived scrutiny scale reliability
- Digital monitoring fairness scale reliability
- Task difficulty manipulation check
- Practice/mastery manipulation check

## Recommended validity checks

- Verify task difficulty categories empirically.
- Test whether social presence increases arousal or perceived scrutiny.
- Test whether performance effects reverse across task difficulty.
- Test whether task mastery moderates audience effects.
- Test whether evaluation pressure predicts performance beyond mere presence.
- Test whether distraction mediates impairment on complex tasks.
