# Measurement Notes

## Groupthink-risk index

A practical index can combine antecedent conditions, symptom indicators, and decision-process failures:

GTR = w1*Cohesion
    + w2*LeadershipDirectiveness
    + w3*Insulation
    + w4*StressLevel
    + w5*ConsensusPressure
    + w6*SelfCensorship
    + w7*Mindguarding
    - w8*DissentVisibility
    - w9*OutsideInformation
    - w10*DecisionSafeguards

## Decision quality

Decision quality should not be inferred from whether a decision later succeeded. A good process can produce a bad outcome under uncertainty, and a bad process can sometimes get lucky. Measure:

- alternative search breadth
- risk analysis quality
- evidence diversity
- forecast calibration
- contingency planning
- implementation risk
- post-decision review quality

## Illusion of unanimity

Measured as the gap between perceived agreement and private agreement:

UnanimityGap = PerceivedConsensus - PrivateAgreement

Large positive values suggest that silence, self-censorship, or status pressure may be inflating apparent consensus.

## Self-censorship probability

Self-censorship is expected to increase with cohesion, leadership directiveness, consensus pressure, stress, and norm enforcement, and decrease with psychological safety, dissent visibility, and leader impartiality.

## Recommended reliability checks

- Cohesion scale reliability
- Leadership directiveness reliability
- Consensus pressure reliability
- Self-censorship reliability
- Mindguarding reliability
- Dissent visibility reliability
- Risk-analysis quality reliability
- Psychological safety reliability
- Safeguard implementation reliability

## Recommended validity checks

- Confirm directive-leadership manipulation increased perceived leader preference.
- Confirm insulation manipulation reduced outside-information access.
- Confirm stress manipulation increased perceived urgency.
- Confirm devil’s advocate intervention increased dissent visibility.
- Test whether safeguards reduce self-censorship before improving decision quality.
- Test whether illusion of unanimity predicts weaker alternative search.
