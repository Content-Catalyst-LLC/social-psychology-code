# Measurement Notes

## Core conflict-intensity model

Conflict intensity can be represented as:

C* = beta0
   + beta1*ResourceCompetition
   + beta2*ZeroSumPerception
   + beta3*IdentitySalience
   + beta4*GroupIdentification
   + beta5*StatusThreat
   + beta6*SymbolicThreat
   + beta7*RealisticThreat
   + beta8*StereotypeEndorsement
   + beta9*IntergroupAnxiety
   + beta10*PerceivedInjustice
   + beta11*Dehumanization
   + beta12*NormOfRetaliation
   - beta13*ContactQuality
   - beta14*CommonGoalSalience
   - beta15*InstitutionalLegitimacy

## Contact-reduction model

Conflict reduction is more likely when contact includes:

- equal status
- cooperative interdependence
- common goals
- institutional support
- repeated interaction
- reduced anxiety
- increased empathy
- reduced threat
- credible norms of nonviolence and respect

## Escalation recurrence

Threat can increase across time when hostile interaction feeds future threat:

T(t+1) = T(t) + lambda*Hostility(t) - delta*Cooperation(t)

Hostility can then respond to threat:

H(t+1) = alpha*Threat(t+1) + beta*IdentitySalience + gamma*RetaliationNorm

## De-escalation recurrence

Conflict can decline when groups cooperate under superordinate goals:

C(t+1) = C(t) - mu*SuperordinateGoal - nu*ContactQuality - omega*InstitutionalLegitimacy

## Recommended reliability checks

- Resource competition scale reliability
- Identity salience reliability

- Status threat reliability

- Stereotype endorsement reliability
- Intergroup anxiety reliability
- Contact quality reliability

## Recommended validity checks

- Confirm competition manipulation increased zero-sum perception.
- Confirm identity-salience manipulation increased group-based self-categorization.
- Confirm contact intervention improved contact quality rather than merely contact quantity.
- Confirm superordinate goal required real cooperation.
- Test whether equal status moderates contact effects.
- Test whether institutional support moderates contact effects.
- Test whether threat predicts hostility after accounting for resource competition.
