# Measurement Notes

## Identity-shift model

Behavior can be modeled as a weighted combination of personal standards and group norms:

B_i = (1 - lambda_i) * PersonalStandard_i + lambda_i * GroupNorm_i

where lambda_i increases as anonymity, crowd immersion, and group identity salience increase.

## Self-awareness

Effective self-awareness can be represented as:

S_i* = S_i - alpha_1*Anonymity_i - alpha_2*CrowdImmersion_i + alpha_3*Accountability_i

## SIDE model logic

Anonymity does not necessarily reduce regulation. It may shift regulation from personal identity to social identity:

PersonalIdentitySalience_i = baseline_personal_i - gamma*Anonymity_i
SocialIdentitySalience_i = baseline_group_i + beta*Anonymity_i*GroupIdentitySalience_i

## Norm-congruent behavior

Norm-congruent behavior is the theoretically central outcome for SIDE designs:

NormCongruence_i = similarity(Behavior_i, SalientGroupNorm_i)

A prosocial group norm can increase prosocial behavior under anonymity.
An antisocial group norm can increase antisocial behavior under anonymity.

## Online behavior

Digital settings require separate measures for:

- pseudonymity
- traceability
- persistence of records
- audience size
- platform moderation visibility
- rule clarity
- community identity
- reputational stakes

## Recommended reliability checks

- Self-awareness scale reliability
- Accountability scale reliability
- Group identity salience scale reliability
- Norm salience and norm clarity reliability
- Moral disengagement scale reliability
- Responsibility diffusion scale reliability

## Recommended validity checks

- Confirm anonymity manipulation affected perceived identifiability.
- Confirm group identity manipulation affected identity salience.
- Confirm norm valence manipulation was perceived as intended.
- Test whether anonymity increases norm-congruent behavior only when group identity/norms are salient.
- Test whether accountability reverses or moderates anonymity effects.
- Test whether digital moderation visibility reduces antisocial behavior without suppressing prosocial dissent.
