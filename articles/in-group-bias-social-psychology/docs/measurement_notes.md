# Measurement Notes

## Bias differential

B_i = R_in,i - R_out,i

where R may represent trust, warmth, empathy, fairness, allocation, cooperation, or forgiveness.

## Moral asymmetry

M_i = blame_out,i - blame_in,i

Positive values indicate harsher blame for outgroup targets than ingroup targets.

## Allocation bias

A_i = allocation_in,i - allocation_out,i

Positive values indicate preferential allocation to ingroup targets.

## Identity salience interaction

outcome_i = beta0 + beta1 * ingroup_target_i + beta2 * identity_salience_i
            + beta3 * ingroup_target_i * identity_salience_i + error_i

The interaction term estimates whether in-group preference grows as identity becomes more salient.

## Threat amplification

B_i = beta0 + beta1 * identity_salience_i + beta2 * perceived_threat_i
      + beta3 * norm_strength_i + beta4 * group_identification_i + error_i

## Institutional accumulation

Even small individual bias differentials can compound over repeated hiring, promotion, discipline, grading, lending, policing, evaluation, or resource allocation decisions.

## Recommended reliability checks

- Internal consistency for identity salience, threat, trust, fairness, empathy, and perceived norm scales
- Test-retest reliability for stable group identification
- Intercoder reliability for institutional decision coding
- Response-time filtering rules documented in advance

## Recommended validity checks

- Compare explicit ratings with behavioral allocation
- Separate in-group love from outgroup hate
- Test whether effects persist under anonymity
- Test scarcity versus abundance conditions
- Test dominant and marginalized group positions separately
- Test whether superordinate identity or contact changes bias differentials
