# Research Protocol: Social Dilemmas

## Purpose

This protocol supports empirical and simulated research on social dilemmas: situations in which individually rational actions produce collectively inferior outcomes. It supports public-goods games, commons dilemmas, resource depletion studies, collective-action designs, institutional-enforcement experiments, and cooperation-under-risk research.

## Core constructs

### Contribution

Amount of endowment allocated to the collective good.

### Extraction

Amount removed from a shared resource.

### Free-riding index

Private benefit retained while benefiting from group contributions.

### Trust

Belief that others will contribute, restrain extraction, comply with rules, or reciprocate cooperation.

### Norm salience

Strength of cooperative expectations made visible in the setting.

### Enforcement credibility

Perceived probability that noncooperation will be detected and sanctioned.

### Institutional legitimacy

Perceived fairness, appropriateness, and acceptability of rules and governance procedures.

### Monitoring strength

Ability of the group or institution to observe behavior.

### Sanction probability and severity

Expected likelihood and cost of punishment for defection or over-extraction.

### Reciprocity expectation

Belief that cooperation will be met with cooperation and defection with sanction or withdrawal.

### Resource stock

State of a shared resource over time.

### Group welfare

Aggregate payoff or welfare produced by the group.

## Recommended designs

1. One-shot public-goods game.
2. Repeated public-goods game with conditional cooperation.
3. Public-goods game with costly punishment.
4. Public-goods game with communication.
5. Commons extraction game with resource regeneration.
6. Institutional legitimacy and enforcement manipulation.
7. Monitoring-strength and transparency manipulation.
8. Social identity and in-group cooperation study.
9. Intergenerational social dilemma design.
10. Polycentric governance and nested-group simulation.

## Measurement cautions

- Distinguish public-goods dilemmas from commons dilemmas.
- Measure expectations about others, not only contributions.
- Include rule legitimacy, not only sanction severity.
- Separate monitoring from punishment.
- Include fairness and distributional asymmetry.
- Track both individual payoff and group welfare.
- In commons designs, model resource stock and regeneration explicitly.
- Avoid treating all collective-action problems as structurally identical.
- Avoid using "tragedy of the commons" as a synonym for all commons governance.

## Ethics

Social dilemma experiments can involve deception, unequal payoffs, punishment, group pressure, and induced scarcity. Researchers should debrief participants, avoid excessive inducements, document payment rules, and interpret noncooperation as behavior under incentives rather than moral character.
